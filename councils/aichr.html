ASEAN Intergovernmental Commission on Human Rights (AICHR)

The AICHR was inaugurated by ASEAN leaders in 2009, reflecting ASEAN’s commitment towards the strengthening of human rights efforts in the region. Until today, the AICHR has not resorted to any public action against human rights violations which occurred within ASEAN. Participants are required to think one step further to better draft policy papers encompassing effective solutions to resolve rights violations while taking into account ASEAN’s stance of non-intervention. The Question of Refugees from Conflict Zones

The plight of Rohingyas has caught the eyes of many around the world, drawing greater scrutiny towards efforts being made to tackle the issue. The escalation of the refugee crisis in Asia in recent decades has become one of the most pressing global issues with millions being forced to flee from their homeland and resettle in foreign countries. While ASEAN efforts to provide financial aid have alleviated some from extreme poverty, millions remain. The nature of such crises could also be marked by years of historical animosity and discrimination against certain communities, making it harder for policies to effectively counter these ingrained stereotypes. In this council, participants will also be guided in the process of crafting solutions to one of the most pertinent issues while learning more about ASEAN’s efforts in championing for human rights.
