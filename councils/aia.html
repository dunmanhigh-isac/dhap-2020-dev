ASEAN Investment Area Council

The ASEAN Investment Area Council (AIA), established by the ASEAN Economic Ministers, is working towards establishing a free and open investment regime. Amid the ever-changing economic landscape, the pursuit of economic integration across ASEAN has been a challenging task thus far. Hence, participants will be challenged to come up with strategies working towards a more competitive investment environment in ASEAN.

The Question of Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) in Southeast Asia

With SMEs playing a vital role in fuelling ASEAN’s economy, the competitiveness of SMEs will be inextricably linked to the competitiveness of ASEAN as a global economic hub. However, there remains a lack of concrete steps to maximise their contributions. In light of the volatile international developments, ASEAN has to tap into every available resource and cannot afford to neglect small and medium enterprises. Participants of AIA must therefore explore the realms of possibilities for greater collaboration between ASEAN’s SMEs, increasing their overall participation in the global value chains. This would also mean challenging participants to suggest ways in elevating SMEs’ level of readiness for integration into the various free trade agreements ASEAN is currently involved in.
