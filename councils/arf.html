ASEAN Regional Forum

Established in 1994, The ASEAN Regional Forum (ARF) brings together 27 member states for dialogue on political and security issues of common interest within the Asia-Pacific. The acknowledgement of its diverse membership has seen ARF advocate a measured approach towards security cooperation and open dialogue on critical issues concerning its member states. This is evident from its adherence to ASEAN’s fundamental principles of consensus and consultation.

The Question of the South China Sea Dispute

The decades-long dispute over the maritime boundaries and islands in the South China Sea is one of the most pressing global security issues, with the Philippines, Vietnam, China, Brunei, and Malaysia laying their respective territorial claims. The South China Sea holds tremendous economic value, as it contains rich reserves of natural resources and hosts about one-third of global shipping lanes. Participants can delve into this complex long-standing issue to come up with a policy paper to manage inter-state tensions over the South China Sea.
