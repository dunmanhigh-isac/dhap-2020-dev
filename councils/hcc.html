Historical Crisis Committee

In this edition of DHAP Writes! 2020, the Historical Crisis Committee (HCC) has undergone a radical change in format, where participants can expect to immerse themselves in a full military experience. Under a dynamic and fast-paced environment, participants will be stretched to think of unconventional methods to manage the crisis in short amounts of time while considering the potential implications of their actions.

The 1967 Arab-Israeli War

The Historical Crisis Committee (HCC) takes participants back to 1967 as military officers of the Arab League. HCC will depict one of the most well-known wars in modern history - The 1967 Arab-Israeli War. Also known as the Third Arab-Israeli War, its outbreak can be attributed to the escalation of tensions as a result of the previous two Arab-Israeli wars as well as underlying hostilities between Israel and its Arab neighbours. With the motives and actions of both sides remaining controversial even after more than fifty years of its conclusion, participants will get the opportunity to put their creativity and strategic thinking to the test by using any available resources to manage the increasingly hostile Israeli actions in the region.
